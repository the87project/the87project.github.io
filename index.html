The 87 What Britain actually thinks — one full double-decker at a time

Put 87 people on one double-decker and ask them the big questions.

A full London double-decker carries 87 people — 40 on the upper deck, 47 below. Every poll here is redrawn as that one busload, seated together by what they actually said. Data from published public polling, primarily YouGov.

About the numbers. Figures are drawn from publicly reported polling (primarily YouGov). Each question is redrawn as one full double-decker bus of 87 people — the capacity of a New Routemaster (40 upper deck, 47 below) — so one passenger represents ~1.15% of respondents; rounding applies, and no group that registered in the source polling is ever rounded off the bus entirely. All commentary is original and independent, and every entry links to its source. Built as a personal data & policy project.
